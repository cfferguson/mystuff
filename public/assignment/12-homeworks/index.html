Note: The \(\chi^2\) value obtained will be referenced by the variable \(\chi^2_{obt}\).
1

  1. We have \[\begin{align} df &= (3-1)\cdot(2-1)\\ &= 2 \end{align}\] yielding 2 degrees of freedom.

  2. We have \(\chi^2 = 3.311\). With \(\chi^2_{obt} = 0.191 > \alpha = 0.01\), we fail to reject \(H_0\) and conclude that sex and opinion of whether or not people can be trusted are likely independent. Specifically, similar percentages of men (\(34.94\)%) and women (\(29.7\)%) felt that people can be trusted.

  3. If \(\alpha = 0.05\), \(\chi^2_{obt} = 0.191\) would still be greater and thus we would still reject \(H_0\).

  4. We have \(\lambda = 0.000\) implying that there is no proportional reduction of error using sex to predict whether or not people can be trusted.
